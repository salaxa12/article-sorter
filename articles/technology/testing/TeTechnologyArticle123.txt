Sony is expected to launch as many as six smartphones in 2023, according to a Reddit user. All of them will be equipped with Snapdragon chipsets. Before reading on, people should take the leak with a grain of salt because its source could not be confirmed.

As per the leak, there are six Sony Xperia phones planned for the year 2023. The model numbers of these devices are PM-142x-BV, PM-143x-BV, PM-144x-BV, PM-145x-BV, PM-146x-BV, and PM-147x-BV. Among these, three devices will feature the Snapdragon 8 Gen 2 mobile platform, two devices will have the Snapdragon 4 Gen 1, and there will be one device featuring the Snapdragon 6 Gen 1.

Sony Xperia Pro-I

It is likely that the Xperia 1 V, Xperia 5 V, and Xperia Pro 2023 may feature the SD8G2 chip. In 2020, Sony launched the Xperia Pro, featuring the Snapdragon 865 chip. The Xperia Pro-I, powered by the Snapdragon 888 was released in 2021. With only two months remaining in this year, it appears that the Japanese tech giant has no plans to release a Snapdragon 8 Gen 1 / 8+ Gen 1 powered Xperia Pro model this year.

The Xperia 10 V could be powered by the SD6G1. There is a possibility that the Xperia Ace IV may feature the SD4G1. In October, the rumor mill spilt some details about the Xperia Ace IV. It is expected to arrive with a 5.5-inch OLED FHD+ display with a 21:9 aspect ratio, SD4G1, 6 GB of RAM, 128 GB of storage, a microSD card slot, a 4,500mAh battery with 18W charging, and a 3.5mm audio jack.

The other SD4G1 phone could be the same Ace model with different branding for markets outside of Japan. The detailed specs of the other Sony phones are not known yet.
